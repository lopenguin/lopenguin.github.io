On SDP Duality

While SDP duality is not philosophically different than duality for linear programs, I initially found it difficult to wrap my head around. This is because many textbooks cover only conic duality, without expliciting stating SDP duality. I've used a number of sources for this writeup, but I found this page particularly helpful.

First, a quick reference table:

PrimalDual
\[\begin{aligned} \min_{X\succeq 0}\ & \langle C, X \rangle\\ \mathrm{s.t.}\ & \langle A_i, X \rangle = a_i,\ i=1,...,n,\\& \langle B_j, X \rangle \geq b_j,\ j=1,...,m, \end{aligned}\]

|

\[\begin{aligned} \max_{\lambda\in\mathbb{R}^m,\ \mu\in\mathbb{R}^n}\ & \sum_{i=1}^n \mu_i a_i\\\mathrm{s.t.}\ & \sum_{i=1}^n \mu_i A_i + \sum_{j=1}^m \lambda_j B_j \succeq C\\& \lambda_j \geq 0,\ j=1,...,m.\end{aligned}\]

|

Really, the dual just "swaps" the constraints and the objective. Part of the magic of the duality is that familiar practioners can just write the dual without thinking. We'll start with a measured approach, and then go over how to just write the dual.
